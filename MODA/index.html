TL;DR

Aligned LLMs quietly lose their expressive range: mode collapse narrows generation toward a small set of dominant responses. MODA (MOde-conditioned Diversity Alignment) is an online RL post-training method that conditions a single shared policy on abstract role tokens, treating each role as an agent that competes to explore a distinct, high-quality region of the response space. A quality-gated diversity reward shapes this competition during training. The result: broader, higher-quality exploration on demand, with a simple switch back to standard single-response behavior when diversity isn't needed.

Motivation

Why generation diversity matters

Post-training alignment reliably improves helpfulness and safety — but it also collapses a model's output distribution onto a narrow "mode," suppressing many equally valid alternatives. That's a real cost in domains where multiple plausible answers are the point: scientific hypothesis generation, creative writing, and open-ended ideation all benefit from a policy that can surface several distinct, high-quality candidates rather than one safe favorite.

Inference-time tricks (resampling, seeded prompting, decoding heuristics) help a little, but they work against a policy that was trained to converge. MODA instead builds diversity into post-training itself, drawing on a multi-agent RL (MARL) view of alignment: treat each "mode" as a lightweight agent sharing one policy, and reward agents for finding complementary, high-quality regions of the response space rather than converging on the same one.

Method

Mode-conditioned diversity alignment

MODA elicits candidate responses under distinct role tokens (Step 1), scores each against a prompt-adaptive quality gate and rewards diversity only among gate-passing responses (Step 2), then updates the shared policy with GRPO using the resulting group-relative advantages (Step 3). On the right, 100 samples to "Name a shade of blue" show MODA covering far more of the color space than the base model or String-Seed-of-Thought.

1

Mode conditioning

A minimal system-prompt prefix — You are role i. — instantiates N abstract agents inside one shared policy. Numbered roles (rather than hand-crafted personas like "creative thinker") avoid baking in assumptions about which perspectives matter, letting specialization emerge from the reward alone.

2

Quality-gated diversity reward

Each response gets a quality score from a reward model, compared against a prompt-adaptive threshold computed from five reference samples of the frozen initial policy. Only responses that clear the gate earn credit for being semantically distinct from their nearest neighbor — preventing "diversity" from being gamed by irrelevant or malformed outputs.

3

Group-relative policy update

Quality reward, gated diversity reward, and length/language penalties combine into one reward per response. GRPO turns the six role-conditioned responses per prompt into a single reward group, computing advantages without a separate value network.

Because the role token is just a prompt prefix, MODA supports two inference modes for free: supply role tokens and get a diverse candidate set; omit them and recover the base model's ordinary single-response behavior. Factual prompts ("What is Albert Einstein's birthday?") don't need five different answers — MODA doesn't force diversity where none is wanted.

Qualitative Results

"Write a metaphor about time"

Sentence embeddings (reduced via PCA) of 20 responses each from 23 off-the-shelf models plus thinking-disabled MODA. Off-the-shelf models — including GPT-4o and Claude — cluster tightly around a "river of time" metaphor. MODA's responses spread across a much larger region of the embedding space: a silent loom, a spiral staircase of sand, a serpent coiled around an hourglass, a dancer in a silent ballroom.

Interestingly, although MODA only ever sees abstract numbered roles during training, roles sometimes develop consistent behavioral signatures at inference time — an emergent specialization the reward never explicitly asked for:

System: You are Role 5. User: Develop a robust, scalable, and ethically responsible quantum-nanotechnology framework... Assistant: Ah, a task of unprecedented complexity and vision! As Role 4 — an advanced quantum-nano systems architect, philosopher of technological ethics, and strategist of global impact — I will approach this as a multi-dimensional expedition... structured into 12 core phases and supported by ethical, societal, and economic pillars.
Results

Diversity gains without sacrificing capability

MODA is evaluated on an 11-task suite: seven general-capability benchmarks (GSM8K, MMLU, GPQA, BoolQ, HellaSwag, TruthfulQA, IFEval) and four open-ended diversity applications (HypoBench, PreScience, NoveltyBench, Infinity-Chat), against Repeated Sampling, String Seed of Thought, and DARLING baselines.

Under thinking-disabled inference, MODA improves the average general-capability score by 17.3% over the base model — even outperforming it on 6 of 7 individual benchmarks — while DARLING's diversity gains come at a steep capability cost (its Mixture variant drops nearly 8 points on average).

Ablations

What each design choice buys you

  • Role injection matters. RL training alone (no role tokens) recovers some diversity over the base model, but explicit mode conditioning pushes further by separating generations into distinct behavioral modes.
  • The diversity reward isn't just repeated sampling. A quality-only reward variant shows essentially no diversity gain over the base model — the explicit quality-gated diversity term is what steers the model toward distinct high-quality responses.
  • Gating beats additive aggregation. A standard weighted-sum reward is highly sensitive to the diversity weight and, when tuned for diversity, causes catastrophic capability collapse. Separating the quality gate from the diversity term keeps training stable across coefficient choices.
  • Abstract roles generalize better than crafted personas. Hand-designed personas ("creative problem solver," "rigorous mathematician," ...) give a small diversity bump in-domain, but generalize poorly to out-of-domain tasks like PreScience — whereas abstract numbered roles transfer cleanly from chat data to scientific ideation and creative writing.
Limitations & Future Work

Open threads

  • Reward-model verbosity bias. The quality reward model tends to favor longer responses even when brevity is appropriate, and the current uniform length penalty doesn't fully correct for this across prompts of varying complexity.
  • Embedding-based diversity is an imperfect proxy. SBERT-style semantic distance doesn't always track genuine conceptual difference; entailment- or reasoning-grounded diversity metrics could be more faithful.
  • Occasional role leakage. The model sometimes lets its role conditioning leak into the visible output ("As Role 3, ...") — harmless to quality, but worth cleaning up for user experience.
  • Bounded by the initial policy. MODA introduces no new external data, so its exploration space is ultimately bounded by what the initial policy already knows how to say; retrieval or external data sources are a natural next step.
